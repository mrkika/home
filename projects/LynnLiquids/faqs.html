Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How do Auctions Work

An auction is a public sale in which goods or services are sold to the highest bidder.

Items such as art, vehicles, antiques, collectibles, and more are sold at auctions.

The terms and conditions are usually available on the auction website or can be requested from the auction house.

To register for an auction, you must fill out a registration form, submit required documents, and pay any registration fees.

FICA (Financial Intelligence Centre Act) documents are required to verify your identity, such as your ID, proof of address, and income.

A bidder number is a unique identifier assigned to you when you register to participate in an auction.

A registration fee is a fee charged to participate in an auction, typically to cover administrative costs.

Registration is not always guaranteed. It depends on the auction's rules and whether your registration meets their criteria.

If you don't purchase anything, the registration fee is usually non-refundable.

If you make a purchase, the registration fee is typically considered part of the cost and is not refunded.

Yes, if you fail to comply with the auction terms, your registration fee may be forfeited.

In most cases, you do need to register to attend an auction. However, some public auctions may allow attendance without registration.

You can view auction items online on the auction platform or at the auction venue prior to the auction start time.

Yes, in some vehicle auctions, you can test drive vehicles before bidding, but check the specific auction's terms.

Auctions usually begin with an introduction, followed by an overview of the items up for bidding.

The starting bid is the minimum amount that an item can be bid on at the start of an auction.

Bidding is the act of offering a price for an item being auctioned in the hopes of winning it.

A reserve price is the minimum amount the seller is willing to accept for an item in an auction.

The selling price is determined by the highest bid made during the auction.

The auctioneer will announce the winning bid at the end of the auction or the item will be marked as sold on the platform.

The auction ends when all items have been sold, or no more bids are received within a specified time frame.

Additional charges may include buyer's premiums, taxes, and administrative fees.

You can pay using the payment methods accepted by the auction platform, such as bank transfer, credit card, or other online methods.

Cash payments may be accepted, depending on the auction's terms and conditions.

Once the auction ends, the availability of the item will be confirmed and marked as sold on the auction platform.
