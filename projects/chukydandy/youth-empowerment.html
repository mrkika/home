Empowering the Future: Hon. Festus Okoh Equips Over 1,000 Youths Across Ika South

Published on May 24, 2025 | By Admin

In a bold and visionary stride toward sustainable youth development, Hon. Festus Okoh, the distinguished representative of Ika South, has launched one of the most impactful empowerment initiatives in the region’s recent history. Over 1,000 young men and women have been equipped with vital vocational skills and startup kits—planting seeds of independence, innovation, and economic revival.

This landmark project, which cut across various sectors such as ICT, tailoring, hairdressing, catering, agribusiness, and artisan crafts, reflects a deep understanding of the economic challenges confronting Nigerian youths today. Rather than offer short-term relief, Hon. Okoh has chosen a path of empowerment that fosters dignity, purpose, and long-term prosperity.

“We are not just training youths; we are giving them tools to become creators of jobs, not seekers of jobs,” Hon. Okoh stated during the official launch ceremony held at the Ika South Civic Centre. The event drew local leaders, community stakeholders, beneficiaries, and representatives of various training bodies, all unified in celebrating a visionary act of service.

Transforming Lives with Real Tools

Each beneficiary of the program received hands-on training led by certified professionals. Upon completion, participants were presented with startup kits tailored to their acquired skills: sewing machines, hairdrying kits, laptops, baking ovens, farm tools, and more. These tools are the first building blocks toward self-employment and community development.

“This initiative has given me a new life,” said Mary, a young caterer. “Now I can bake for events and even teach others. I’m forever grateful to Hon. Okoh.”

Beyond Empowerment: Building a Legacy

What sets this program apart is its holistic vision. Beyond the tools, Hon. Okoh is advocating for continuous mentorship and support networks that ensure these businesses don’t just start—but thrive. Local cooperatives are being formed to assist with funding, marketing, and collaborative growth among youth entrepreneurs.

In a country where youth unemployment remains one of the most pressing challenges, this initiative stands as a beacon of hope and a model for national replication. It is more than a political gesture; it is a moral commitment to the future of Ika South.

“This is more than empowerment — it’s a movement. We are raising a generation of builders, dreamers, and doers.” — Hon. Festus Okoh
