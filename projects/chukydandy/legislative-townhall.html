Legislative Town Hall: Hon. Festus Okoh Engages Constituents on Policy and Progress

Published on May 24, 2025 | By Admin

In a bold demonstration of transparency and participatory governance, Hon. Festus Okoh recently hosted a well-attended Legislative Town Hall Meeting with constituents across Ika South. The interactive session, held at the Ika South Community Hall, brought together local leaders, professionals, youth groups, women associations, and everyday citizens eager to connect directly with their representative.

The purpose of the town hall was clear: to provide a platform for constituents to voice concerns, share feedback, and learn firsthand about the legislative activities being pursued on their behalf. Hon. Okoh, known for his people-first approach, presented updates on key bills currently under review and invited robust dialogue on pressing issues affecting the constituency.

Bridging the Gap Between Lawmakers and the People

Hon. Okoh discussed proposed bills related to local economic development, youth employment, agricultural innovation, and rural infrastructure. He broke down legislative terms in simple, relatable language—ensuring everyone in attendance could understand and engage meaningfully.

More than a monologue, the event was a dialogue. Attendees raised questions about healthcare access, education reform, road repairs, security, and women’s representation in governance. Hon. Okoh listened attentively and responded with detailed updates, proposed timelines, and actionable steps already underway.

“This is not just my office, it is our office,” he declared. “You elected me to speak for you, but I cannot speak well unless I also listen well.”

Accountability in Action

A standout moment came when Hon. Okoh unveiled a constituency report booklet that outlined every legislative motion he has sponsored, projects initiated, and results achieved over the past year. This level of openness was widely praised by participants, many of whom called for the institutionalization of town halls as quarterly events.

In addition, the event featured booths for real-time issue documentation, where constituents could register complaints, propose ideas, or seek constituency-specific support. Representatives from education, health, agriculture, and youth affairs were also present to address sector-specific concerns.

“Democracy thrives not in silence, but in dialogue. The people must be heard — and I will keep this door open as long as I serve.” — Hon. Festus Okoh
